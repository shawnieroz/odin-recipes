Banana Pudding Recipe

This luscious treat evokes nostalgic reminiscences from childhood, boasts a remarkably simple preparation method, and has the potential to be the ultimate delight on a sunny day. Crafting your own banana pudding yields a visually appealing spectacle when presented in a trifle bowl, yet it remains equally delectable when served in a regular bowl or casserole dish. For those with a penchant for awe-inspiring desserts, I highly recommend exploring my repertoire of French macarons, indulgent bananas foster, or the timeless classic creme brulee!

    Ingredients:

  • 2 cups whole milk
  • 14 oz sweetened condensed milk 1 can
  • 5 oz instant vanilla pudding mix one package
  • 3 cups heavy cream divided
  • 3 tbsp granulated sugar divided
  • 3 tsp vanilla extract divided
  • 1 box vanilla wafer cookies 16-oz.
  • 5 bananas sliced into coins
  1. In a large bowl whisk together the milk and sweetened condensed milk then sprinkle in the instant pudding and mix until completely incorporated with no lumps remaining. You can do this by hand or with an electric mixer. Refrigerate for 5 minutes or until set.
  2. Add 3 cups of whipped cream with two tablespoons of sugar and 2 teaspoons of vanilla to a bowl then mix on high until stiff peaks from.
  3. Remove pudding from the refrigerator then fold the whipped cream into it.
  4. Add a layer of cookies to the bottom of your trifle dish or a larger bowl. Place a quarter of the pudding mixture over then smooth using a spatula. Add another layer of cookies then place a row of cookies facing the horizontal edge of your dish. Add a layer of banana slices on top then add another quarter of the pudding mixture and smooth.
  5. Continue layering with cookies, bananas and pudding until complete. Alternate a row of banana slices with the cookies you're pressing against the wall of the trifle dish. Chill for three hours.
  6. For the topping whip cup of cup of cream with a tablespoon of sugar and a teaspoon of vanilla to stiff peaks. Pile on top and crumble the remaining cookies over then serve.
